Our Story

More than just a shop - we're a legacy of passion, community, and comic book love.

Meet Uncle Roger

It all started in 1985 when Roger Martinez, fresh out of college with a degree in art history and a collection of over 3,000 comics, decided to turn his passion into a profession. With a small loan and a big dream, he opened the doors to what would become the region's most beloved comic shop.

"Everyone called me 'Uncle Roger' because I treated every young collector like family," Roger recalls. "I'd help kids find their first comic, explain the difference between ages, and always made sure everyone felt welcome. That spirit hasn't changed in 40 years."

Today, while Roger has semi-retired (you'll still find him behind the counter most Saturdays!), the shop is run by his daughter Maria and a team of equally passionate comic enthusiasts who carry on the tradition of community, expertise, and love for the medium.

Our Journey

1985 - The Beginning

Uncle Roger opens the shop in a small 400 sq ft storefront with just 500 comics and a dream. The first customer was a 10-year-old looking for Amazing Spider-Man #252.

1992 - First Expansion

The comic boom of the early 90s allows us to move to a larger location. We add gaming tables and start hosting our first tournaments.

1999 - Surviving the Crash

When the speculator bubble bursts, many shops close. We survive by focusing on what matters: community and genuine collectors.

2008 - The Comic Renaissance

Comic book movies bring new fans to the hobby. We launch our subscription box service and double our customer base.

2015 - Maria Takes the Helm

Roger's daughter Maria, who grew up in the shop, officially becomes manager. She modernizes operations while keeping the family spirit alive.

2024 - 40 Years Strong

We celebrate four decades of serving collectors! Now in our largest location yet, with over 50,000 comics and a community that spans generations.

Our Mission

Preserve the Art

Comics are an art form deserving of respect and preservation. We're committed to maintaining the history of the medium and educating new generations about its cultural significance.

Build Community

A comic shop should be more than retail - it should be a gathering place. We foster connections between collectors, creators, and readers of all ages and backgrounds.

Champion Creators

We actively support indie publishers, local artists, and emerging talent. Every voice deserves a chance to be heard, and every story deserves to be told.
